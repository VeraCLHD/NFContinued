For gout sufferers, the uric acid-lowering properties of dairy may be desirable.For years, doctors advised gout patients to avoid all high-purine foods, but one large Harvard study showed gout was linked to increased meat and fish consumption; the vegetables gout sufferers were told to avoid (mushrooms, peas, beans, lentils, and cauliflower) were found to be protective.Eating an alkaline, vegetarian diet may remove uric acid from the body.
