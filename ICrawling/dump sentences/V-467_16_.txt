For example, before starting someone at moderate risk on a cholesterol-lowering statin drug, a physician might ideally say something like, “You should know that for [folks in your situation], the number of individuals who must be treated with a statin to prevent one death from a cardiovascular event such as a heart attack or stroke—is generally between 60 and 100, which means that if I treated 60 people in your position, 1 would benefit and 59 would not.
