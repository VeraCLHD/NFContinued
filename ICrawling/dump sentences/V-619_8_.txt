They took a bunch of young women at high risk for breast cancer—meaning they had a suspicious breast biopsy (showing either precancerous changes, or carcinoma in situ), or already had breast cancer in the other breast—and gave them a teaspoon of ground flaxseeds every day for a year, before getting repeat needle biopsies to see if there were any changes.Yes, there are lignans in sesame seeds, nuts, whole grains, legumes, certain fruits, and veggies, but they’re most concentrated in flax seeds.
