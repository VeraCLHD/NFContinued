Well-funded associations can influence legislation, regulations, dietary guidelines, nutritional research, and public messaging.The sugar, salt, egg, poultry, beef, pork, fish, juice, dairy, and snack food industries have been accused of downplaying the risks of their products, while the nutritional supplements industry has frequently been charged with exaggerating its marketing claims.Industry associations often underwrite research studies, sometimes producing a “funding effect,” the skewing of results favorable to research funders.
