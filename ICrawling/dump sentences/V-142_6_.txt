We know vinegar can activate AMPK in human cells, but is the dose one might get sprinkling it on a salad enough?If you take endothelial cells—blood-vessel-lining cells—from umbilical cords after babies are born and expose them to various levels of acetate, which is what the acetic acid in vinegar turns into in our stomach, it appears to take a concentration of at least 100 to really get a significant boost in AMPK.
