Yes, eggs are by far the #1 source of cholesterol in the American diet, but letters to the editor, like this one, protested that “dietary cholesterol may have very little impact on [blood] cholesterol” levels—citing this study, published in 1971, performed on eight people.But, if you look at dozens of studies all put together, covering hundreds of study subjects, we find that “[blood] cholesterol concentration is clearly increased by added dietary cholesterol.”
