In one study, daily flaxseed was shown to reduce high blood pressure as good or better than any other nutritional intervention and as effectively as many hypertension drugs, which can have serious side effects.Those interested in adding flaxseed to their diet should note that lignans are activated by gut bacteria, so taking antibiotics may impair the production of the lignans.In addition to their specific cancer- and diabetes-fighting properties, flaxseeds are an excellent source of omega-3 fatty acids, iron, zinc, calcium, protein, potassium, magnesium, folate, soluble fiber and even boron.
