So, you can understand the skepticism in the medical community regarding claims about vitamin D, which is now enjoying its moment in the sun.Having a half-billion dollar vitamin D supplement industry doesn’t help matters, not to mention the highly lucrative vitamin D-testing industry that loves to talk about the studies suggesting having higher vitamin D levels may reduce the risk of heart disease, and cancer, and diabetes, autoimmune diseases, and infections.But most of this research stems from observational studies—meaning studies that correlate higher D levels in the blood with lower disease risk.
