“Numerous studies have suggested a decrease in atherosclerotic disease risk [heart disease, our #1 killer] in vegan and vegetarian individuals compared to omnivores; [but, we’ve just assumed this was due to reduced intake] of dietary cholesterol and saturated fat…”But, what these researchers found was that within 24 hours of carnitine consumption—eating a sirloin steak, taking a carnitine supplement—certain gut bacteria metabolize the carnitine to a toxic substance called trimethylamine, which then gets oxidized in our liver to TMAO (trimethylamine-n-oxide), which then circulates throughout our bloodstream.
