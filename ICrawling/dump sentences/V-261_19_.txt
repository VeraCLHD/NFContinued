We know that mercury in fish can cause neurological damage, such as negative effects on Alzheimer disease, memory loss, and autism as well as depression.So, the increased risk of suicide among persons with a high fish intake might be attributable to the harmful effects of mercury in fish.The big Harvard cohorts found similar results; hundreds of thousands followed for up to 20 years, and no evidence that taking fish oil or eating fish lowered risk of suicide, with a trend towards even higher suicide mortality.What about for the treatment of depression?
