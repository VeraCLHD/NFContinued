This hypothetical risk was raised as far back as the 60s in the New England Journal, where it was pondered whether the age-old custom of adding nutmeg to eggnog arose from the “psychopharmacological effects described” in cases of nutmeg intoxication.Such cases evidently go back to the 1500s, where it was used as an abortifacent, to induce a miscarriage, and in the 1960s, as a psychotropic drug.
