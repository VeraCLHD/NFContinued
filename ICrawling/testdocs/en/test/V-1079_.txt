Can our cholesterol be too low, though? In the 1980s, some studies came out showing that many people dying from cancer have really low cholesterol. There are two possible explanations for this correlation: the cancer led to the low cholesterol; or, maybe, low cholesterol led to cancer. That wouldn’t be good. To this day, there are doctors telling people not to let their cholesterol get too low based on that data. Are they right? Can our bad cholesterol—LDL—be too low?
